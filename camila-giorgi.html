Camila Giorgi Career:

Camila Giorgi has had a successful career as a tennis player. Her mother, Antonnela, was passionate about sports and may have instilled it in her. She first held a racket at five and has not let go 25 years later. Camila was spotted by Nick Bollettieri, one of the renowned tennis coaches in Italy, thanks to her style when she interacted with Adriano Panatta. Nick trained her for seven months, which made all the difference. 

In her junior years, she made it to the final stage of the Nike Junior Tour in 2015, even though she lost to Zuzana Luknarova. Other milestones in these years include making it to the finals at the Sey Development Cup and round of 16 at the Astrid Bowl in Belgium. 

She began with $10k tournaments in her early professional carer, reached two semifinals in Jakarta and Baku, and later tried a $25k tournament, closing 2016 with ten wins, seven losses, and a 944th WTA ranking. 2009 saw her play several tournaments in France. She ended 2009 with 33 wins, 12 defeats and a 285th ranking. 2010 wasn’t good for her as she sustained first-round losses in three months and was defeated on her first attempt at qualifying for the Grand Slam tour. 

In 2011, she had her grand slam and top 150 debuts, and in 2012 qualified for Wimbledon Championships, where she went strong till the fourth round. She also made her top 100 debuts. In 2013, she made it to the fourth round of the US Open and a year later had her breakthrough by making it to two WTA finals and the Top 50. 

She won her first WTA title in 2015 and made it to the top 30. However, in 2016 she lost several games and was out of the top 50. 2017 had mixed results, while in 2018, she won her second WTA title and made it back to the top 30. 2019 saw her out of the top 50, even though she managed two WTA tournament finals. 2021 was better as she reached the Olympics quarterfinals, got her first WTA Masters Title and made it back to the top 40.
