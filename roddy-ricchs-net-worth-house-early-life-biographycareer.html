Early life

Rodrick Wayne Moore Jr. was born on October 22, 1998, in Compton, California.

Raised in a Christian household, he attended Compton High School.

Roddy Ricch started rapping at eight and performed for Kendrick Lamar at twelve.

He seriously began making beats at the age of 16.

While in Compton, he was a member of the Park Village Compton Crips, evident from his “C” tattoos.

During the release of his second commercial mixtape, “Feed Tha Streets II,” he had a brief jail stint.

Moore’s musical influences included Lil Wayne, Young Thug, Future, and Meek Mill.

Career

In November 2017, Roddy Ricch dropped his first mixtape, Feed Tha Streets, featuring tracks like “Chase the Bag” and “HoodRicch.”

Praised by Nipsey Hussle and Meek Mill, he also released the unofficial EP “Be 4 Tha Fame” that year.

Roddy penned “Die Young” in memory of a friend who died in a race. The music video gained millions of views.

September 2018 saw the release of “Ricch Forever,” and Meek Mill had him as a special guest in Philadelphia.

He featured on Meek Mill’s “Championship” alongside Future and Young Thug.

In February 2019, Roddy won a Grammy for Best Rap Performance for his feature on Nipsey Hussle’s “Racks in the Middle.”

He released “Out Tha Mud” and “Ballin” in 2019 and “Big Stepper” in October 2019.

His debut studio album, “Please Excuse Me for Being Antisocial,” arrived in December 2019, topping the Billboard 200.

The album included “The Box,” his highest-charting single, and “High Fashion” with Mustard.

He featured in Meek Mill’s “Letter To Nipsey” and A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie’s “Numbers” song.

Roddy collaborated with NLE Choppa on “Walk Em Down” and DaBaby on “Rockstar.”

He also appeared on Pop Smoke’s “The Woo,” Cordae’s “Gifted,” and Don Toliver’s “Lemonade.”

In 2021, he debuted “Heartless” during a live performance and released “4 Da Gang” with 42 Dugg.

Roddy was featured on DJ Khaled’s “Body in Motion” alongside Bryson Tiller and Lil Baby.

He collaborated with Birdman on “Stunnaman” featuring Lil Wayne, followed by the lead single “Late at Night” from his second studio album.

His latest collaboration with Bino Rideaux is titled “Lemme Find Out,” and he joined Future and Gunna on the “Too Easy” remix.
