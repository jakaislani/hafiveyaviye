9 Best Water Enhancers [Taste Test!]

June 2024 · 6 minute read

If you’re a person who’s always complained that “water tastes like nothing,” the best water enhancers make staying hydrated a lot tastier. They’re great for school lunch boxes and camping trips. Leave a few in your car or at your desk at work to spice up those 64 ounces of H2O you’re obligated to drink every day. But what’s the best water flavoring of them all? That’s what we wanted to find out.

Whether you crave the taste of iced tea, aguas frescas, lemonade, or even candy-flavored beverages (whatever that entails), we have a water flavoring for you. Over the course of two separate taste tests, we tried a wide selection of water flavor enhancers, both generic and name brand, in search of flavored water that tastes bolder and better than bottled beverages—and not like a cheaper, diluted version of the real thing. After mixing numerous powders and liquids into our office’s filtered tap water, we found the nine best water flavor enhancers to help you stay hydrated.

Best Low Calorie

Wyler’s Light Strawberry LemonadeAmazonDollar Tree

Strawberry can go so wrong in artificially flavored beverages, but Wyler’s Light dodges all the mishaps with this strawberry lemonade water flavoring. For a low-calorie water enhancer, I can barely taste the artificial sweetener. These packets of powder make water taste a little closer to an agua fresca than actual lemonade, but I prefer that to the taste of overtart, lip-pursing lemon juice. Overall, the flavor is very light, bright, and refreshing. Even at the bottom of the list, Wyler’s Light Strawberry Lemonade is still one of the best water flavorings you can buy. —Ariana Losch

Best Sugar-Free

Bolero Advanced Hydration Sugar-Free Iced Tea SachetsAmazon

If you know me, you know I hate things that are sugar free. Not foods that are naturally sugar free, but processed sugar free products. They always have such a weird taste. I’d rather just not eat the thing than eat a sugar free, chemical-y tasting, diet version of the thing. Bolero Advanced Hydration Sugar-Free Tea Sachets (aka, powder packets) taste good, though. I tried them at a food convention and was shocked by the flavor. They make flavored water that actually tastes like a lightly sweetened tea and not a diet drink. Good job, Bolero, for making one of the best water enhancers out there. —Jordan Myrick

Best Candy Flavor

Starburst Blue RaspberryAmazonTarget

I’ll admit, I was skeptical of Starburst water flavorings. Why would I want to drink candy? The cherry flavor didn’t really hit for me, but oh man, blue raspberry? It’s a game changer. Vibrant and bold, this powdered water enhancer has just the right balance of sweet and tart to make a candy-flavored drink taste actually refreshing rather than cloying. The concentrated blue raspberry flavor actually reminds me more of a blue Jolly Rancher than a blue Starburst. Many of you recommended the Sonic water flavorings in the comments. While Sonic water enhancers, Ocean Water included, were a little too forgettable for us to recommend here, we still found a blue flavored water that we really like. —Ariana Losch
